MARS-CL — continual learning without stored data

A neural system that learns 10 Fashion-MNIST classes in 5 sequential tasks without storing a single training image. Class prototypes are public GloVe word vectors ("semantic anchors"); the past is retained as tiny per-class feature statistics replayed as "dreams" (parametric sleep) on a frozen random backbone. Everything below runs in your browser, in plain JavaScript — the exact weights trained in PyTorch. Paper & code.

1 · Learn tasks one by one

Each task adds two classes. Click through the sequence and watch the accuracy on earlier tasks: it barely moves. There is no replay buffer — old classes survive as word anchors + ~16 KB of dreamed statistics.

2 · Classify test images

Click any image (never seen in training). Greyed-out images belong to classes the system has not learned yet at this point of the sequence.

3 · What just happened

The image went through a frozen random CNN (never trained — measured to beat a task-trained one), was projected into GloVe word space, and routed to the nearest word anchor; that class's small "pod" produced the final answer. Nearest-word routing is the whole class-incremental mechanism: new class = new word, old anchors never move. Full study: whitepaper (77.6 ± 1.0% class-IL, statistically equivalent to a 200-image replay buffer, ~30% less forgetting, 5 seeds).
